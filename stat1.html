Predicting Roadkill Level in Hongcheon, Gangwondo, Korea

This dashboard shows the roadkill points that occurred in Hongcheon, Gangwon-do, Korea, during 2020, 2021, and 2022.





1. Figure

[Fig. LASSO regression features by importance]




2. Goal


The ultimate goal is not only to predict roadkill risk levels (low, medium, high) in Hongcheon, but also to analyze the environmental and infrastructural characteristics of high-risk grids, providing deeper insights into why incidents occur more frequently.

Explanatory Variables

Variable Description
national_highway_rk Roadkill count on national, provincial, and highway roads
white_rk Roadkill count on ordinary/local roads (non-national/provincial/highway)
corridor_count Number of ecological corridors within the grid
min_dist_to_corridor Minimum distance (m) to the nearest ecological corridor
wild_boar_count Scaled estimate of wild boar distribution in the grid
roe_deer_count Scaled estimate of roe deer distribution in the grid
white_road_grade Categorical index (0–4) describing road presence and surrounding land use
speed_proxy Proxy for speed limits based on road type composition (highway, national)

3. Methodology & Summary

  • With the SGDClassifier, clear overfitting was observed between the training and validation sets.
  • The neural network with two hidden layers trained using SGD achieved 100% accuracy on both the training and validation data.
  • Based on this stability, the neural network model was applied to predict Hongcheon grids, identifying the following as high-risk areas (level 2):
  • grid_333
  • grid_364
  • grid_494
  • grid_526

These results highlight specific locations in Hongcheon that warrant further attention for roadkill mitigation and conservation planning.
